#ENDCHILDFOODPOVERTY

by Adam Perry

2020 was one of the darkest years in many people's lives. A time of struggle and chaos of which many had never experienced before. With the uncertainty around the Coronavirus pandemic, Marcus Rashford MBE has provided a beacon of hope for a nation in distress, leading the charge to feed the children of England.

With families struggling and the government unwilling to assist by cancelling the food voucher scheme for the summer holidays, Rashford petitioned the government to making a U-turn on their decision, helping to feed the nation's children over the summer.

The issue of providing meals for children came to the fore once again in the lead up to October half term as the government insisted they would not fund free school meals.

Communities across the country began to come together to help provide meals for struggling families, with Marcus Rashford taking to Twitter to share places which were offering free or discounted food.

Food insecurity, the inability to secure food of suffient quantity and quality, has been one of the many issues exacerbated by the Covid-19 pandemic, especially with the rise in unemployment.

"Adults who were working in February 2020 but who reported being unemployed in May or July were about 2.5 times more likely to be experiencing food insecurity than those who remained in work."

The Food Foundation - September 2020

The unemployment rate in the UK rose 0.9% between March 2020 and September 2020, contributing to an influx in food insecurity. The furlough scheme helped to protect some people against food insecurity but there was still a rise in food insecurity in comparison to those who remained in full employment.
